What is a Credit Union?

"A credit union is a group of people who save together and lend to each other at a fair and reasonable rate of interest."

While money is the medium, people are the real asset of the Credit Union. The Credit Union helps the people to help themselves and the income from loans and investments pays for necessary operational expenses and the building up of Credit Union reserves. The balance goes back to the members as a dividend on their savings and/or as a rebate on loan interest paid.

Credit unions offer members the chance to have control over their own finances by making their own savings work for them. Every credit union is owned by the members — the people who save and borrow with it — people like you.

 

Our Aims

  1. Promote thrift in the community
  2. Encourage savings
  3. Lend to members at a reasonable rate
  4. Help members to manage their money wisely

 

How are we regulated?

Cahir Credit Union and indeed all Credit Unions are subject to various levels of supervision and regulation. Credit Unions in the Republic of Ireland are primarily regulated by the Central Bank.

Credit Unions affiliated to the Irish League of Credit Unions are subject to audits by field officers from the monitoring section of the Irish League of Credit Unions.

The financial affairs of the credit union are subject to annual audit by our registered auditor who reports directly to the members.

 

The Irish League of Credit Unions

Credit Unions are affiliated to the Irish League of Credit Unions, which is the central governing body of Irish Credit Unions. The League is governed by a Board of Directors elected at the annual convention to which all affiliated Credit Unions send two voting delegates.

While each Credit Union is autonomous, all Credit Unions operate under the terms of the Credit Union Act 1997 as amended and under the "Standard Rules of Credit Unions" copies of which are available at your Credit Union.

Our Services

Loans

No loan is too small for your local Credit Union. Loan applications can be made for a wide variety of purposes including Cars, Home Improvement, Education, Insurance, Holidays, Christmas expenses and others.

To apply for your first loan you must establish a savings record for at least ten weeks and have made a lodgement each week for that period.

Learn more »

 

Savings

Your savings are protected under the Government's Deposit Protection Scheme up to a maximum of €100,000 per member.

Saving with the Credit Union not only allows you to accumulate a lump sum but also opens the door to all the membership benefits that the credit union has to offer.

Remember: One of the criteria's for applying for your first loan is regular consistent saving for three months so get saving as you never know when you might need your first loan!

Paying over the phone by Debit Card

You can pay your loan or add to your savings by phoning us with your Debit Card Details. You can call in at a later date to update your Credit Union Book.

Please note: €500 maximum payment can be made by Debit Card. We do not accept credit cards.
